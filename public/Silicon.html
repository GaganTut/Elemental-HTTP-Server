Atomic number 14

Silicon is present in the sun and stars and is a principal component of a class of meteorites known as aerolites. Silicon makes up 25.7%25 of the earth's crust by weight%2C and is the second most abundant element%2C exceeded only by oxygen. It is found largely as silicon oxides such as sand (silica)%2C quartz%2C rock crystal%2C amethyst%2C agate%2C flint%2C jasper and opal. Silicon is found also in minerals such as asbestos%2C feldspar%2C clay and mica.
